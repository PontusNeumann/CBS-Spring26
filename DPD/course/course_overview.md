## Course Description

During the course, students develop analytical skills and abilities to assess market developments, improve their capabilities to engage in individual and group decision-making under uncertainty, and solve specific business problems. Upon completion of the course, students will be able to develop and present concrete solutions to market problems and advise firms about how to deal with information technology challenges and opportunities.

## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of the course, students are able to:

1. **LO1** — Explain key concepts related to the role of data, digital platforms, and digitalization.
2. **LO2** — Explain how data, digital platforms, and digitalization influence markets and firms through examples based on business cases or sectoral analyses.
3. **LO3** — Use a set of theoretical tools to analyse the key economic, managerial, and societal impacts of data, digital platforms, and digitalization.
4. **LO4** — Evaluate and propose innovative solutions to business problems arising in data, digital platforms, and digitalization, considering the interplay of economic, managerial, and societal factors.

## Learning Activities

In the first session, students decide on the business problem for the project they prepare for the exam. In the following sessions, students receive feedback on how the topics of each session relate to their project through a set of questions presented in the slides that are discussed in the corresponding session. The option of in-person meetings for exam preparation is available throughout the semester.

## Examination Paper Information

### Format

A written project / take-home assignment, **individual exam**, max **10 pages**. Students select a business problem in the first session and develop the analysis over the course of the semester. Each session provides structured feedback questions in the lecture slides to guide how the session topics relate to the chosen project. In-person consultation meetings are offered to support exam preparation.

### LO Alignment

The exam tests the ability to:

- Apply concepts from LO1–LO4 to analyse a concrete business problem.
- Demonstrate understanding of how data, platforms, and digitalization create or destroy value.
- Use theoretical frameworks (e.g. network effects, transaction-cost theory, agency theory, dynamic capabilities, platform economics) to generate insights.
- Propose and evaluate solutions accounting for economic, managerial, and societal dimensions.

## Assignment Instructions (from in-class slides)

Home written assignment, written product, individual exam (max 10 pages):

- Introduce a specific business problem to address.
  - **Traditional firm:** digital transformation, e.g. specific business processes or new business activities.
  - **Digital firm:** digital transformation in the industry, market dynamics, new barriers, and similar.
- Address: how do theories advise managers to deal with the problem? What are the consequences for the firm?
- Be analytical. Use arguments from specific sources and **mention the academics' names**.
- Write in a clear and concise manner.
- **No use of m-dash.**

Course contact: ic.digi@cbs.dk.
